About Evan

About me:

I graduated from Ball State University in 2019 with a major in Computer Science, a minor in History, and having completed the Honors College curriculum and Thesis. During my time at BSU, I immersed myself into the logic and analytical problem-solving field that is coding, learning new languages and fundamentals for a wide array of abilities and conceptual understandings. I was intrigued by the field because I grew up wanting to make videogames and once I started learning, I was blown away with the possibilities of programs and how they are built.

After graduating, I worked a part time job at home while reviewing my school projects and learning whenever possible. I knew something was missing in my education, as I was troubled by the lack of professional experience to gain from college. A friend of mine introduced me to Eleven Fifty Academy after I told her about my interest in growing more as a developer and pushing the bounds of my knowledge. As a current student of Eleven Fifty's .NET Software Development course, I am learning at an accelerated pace and I enjoy every unit of knowledge pumped into my brain from this wonderful course.
